How Much Does an SMSF Cost Per Year? (2026 Guide)

Updated July 2026  ·  By MySuperChoices

One of the most common misconceptions about SMSFs is that they're "free" beyond the setup costs. In reality, running an SMSF has significant ongoing annual expenses — and many of them are mandatory regardless of your fund's size or performance. Understanding these costs is essential before you decide whether an SMSF makes financial sense for your situation.

Total annual SMSF costs in 2026 typically range from $2,500 for a very lean DIY setup to $15,000+ for complex funds with property, multiple members, and ongoing financial advice. The "typical" fund with professional admin sits at around $3,500–$5,000 per year.

Mandatory Costs — You Can't Avoid These

These costs apply to every SMSF, every year, regardless of complexity or balance:

Cost Item 2026 Amount Notes
ASIC corporate trustee fee$59/yrOnly if you have a corporate trustee (recommended)
Independent audit$300–$800Required by law; must be done by an approved SMSF auditor
ATO supervisory levy$259/yrPaid when lodging your annual return
Annual tax return (lodgement)$500–$2,000Depends on accountant; more complex funds cost more
Financial statements preparation$500–$1,500Often bundled with tax return by accountant

At the lowest end — using a DIY accounting platform and a low-cost auditor — your mandatory costs could be kept to around $1,200–$1,800 per year. Most trustees who use a professional accountant will pay $2,500–$4,000 just for these non-negotiable items.

Three Real-World Cost Scenarios

Scenario 1: DIY with Accounting Software (~$1,500/yr)

You use an SMSF accounting platform like Mclowd (free tier) or BGL at the basic level, prepare your own workpapers, use a low-cost online auditor, and lodge via a tax agent. You hold a simple portfolio of ETFs in a single brokerage account. This is achievable but requires substantial time and a good understanding of SMSF compliance rules. Most practitioners do not recommend this approach unless you have an accounting background.

Scenario 2: Typical Professionally Managed Fund (~$3,500–$5,000/yr)

An accountant handles your financial statements, tax return, and coordinates the audit. You use a mid-tier administration platform. You don't have a financial adviser but review your investment strategy annually. This is where the majority of SMSFs sit, and is the cost basis used in most break-even calculations.

Scenario 3: Complex Fund with Property and Adviser (~$8,000–$15,000/yr)

Your SMSF holds direct property (with or without an LRBA), multiple asset classes, and you have an ongoing financial adviser relationship. The LRBA itself requires annual loan statements and additional accounting work. Your adviser reviews your strategy quarterly. At this level, your fund generally needs to be above $800,000–$1 million to justify the cost.

New in 2026: AML/KYC Requirements

From 2026, SMSF trustees face new Anti-Money Laundering and Know Your Customer obligations when dealing with designated services. This adds minor ongoing compliance costs — typically absorbed into existing accountant or adviser fees, but worth confirming with your service provider.

Is the Cost Worth It?

The cost is worth it when your fund balance is high enough that the percentage-equivalent fee is lower than what you'd pay at an industry fund — typically above $350,000–$500,000 for a lean SMSF, or $600,000+ for a fully managed setup.

But cost alone isn't the right lens. The better question is: does the investment strategy you're pursuing inside the SMSF — direct property, business premises, or specific assets — generate enough additional value to justify the total cost? For many high-balance members, the answer is yes. For members who simply want a diversified balanced portfolio, a low-cost industry fund like Hostplus Indexed Balanced will almost always win on net returns after fees.
